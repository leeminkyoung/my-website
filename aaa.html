Building Amazon Ads's 1st-Party Learning Console

Amazon Ads Academy

To help advertisers navigate Amazon's vast suite of ad products, we initially operated a Learning Console. As the platform's limitations became evident, I rebuilt it as a first-party system, Amazon Ads Academy, to deliver a cohesive, branded, and scalable learning experience.

My Role
Sole designer in the project: concept development, service design, UX/UI design, prototyping. Cross-team collaboration with engineering and marketing to align design intent, technical feasibility, and delivery.
Impact
Increased unique weekly visitors by 85.4%
Increased unique registered users by 208.1%
Increased weekly engagements by 24.5%
Set the foundation for UX writing guidelines
Enabled future personalization and improved content discovery

Previous Challenges

1. Visual similarity with the Amazon Ads
  • The Console's navigation and visuals closely mimicked the main website, causing cognitive overlap
  • This visual similarity led to brand confusion and user disorientation
2. Content Overload & Maintenance Burden
  • Overly dense text and repetitive imagery resulted in an overwhelming and confusing user experience
  • This structure created significant internal challenges for partners maintaining and updating the site
3. Poor Information Hierarchy
  • The linear content flow and repeated layouts failed to establish a clear information hierarchy
  • This rigidity resulted in measurably low user engagement and shorter session durations
